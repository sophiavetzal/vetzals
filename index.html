Autonomous vehicle final lab

C++ Linux Commands Microsoft 365 Python

Applied knowledge from the entire course to design a self-driving algorithm for a vehicle where it can avoid obstacles and choose the best direction of travel. First, a wall-following controller was designed that enables the vehicle to drive along a hallway by following two side walls. The code was then further developed to use the largest gap in front of the vehicle to find the best direction of travel for the vehicle. Two parallel virtual barrier lines, one on each side of the direction of travel, are then found to separate the vehicle from obstacles in its environment. These virtual barrier lines are obtained by formulating and solving a Quadratic Optimization with Linear Inequality constraints. This is done with quadprog. The objective is to maximize the distance between the virtual barrier lines, while keeping all obstacle points behind them. The wall-following controller from the earlier algorithm is used to center the vehicle between the two barrier lines found from solving the optimization problem. This was done in both a simulation and real environment.
